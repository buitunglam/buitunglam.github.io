At this time, except for parts of the central and southern Appalachians and perhaps New England, a general coating to an inch of snow is forecast. This will be due to a lack of moisture, insufficient cold air at the onset and no organized storm along the leading press of cold air.

Little to no snow is likely to accumulate in the swath from Roanoke, Virginia, to Washington, D.C., Baltimore, Philadelphia and New York City. All or most of the precipitation in this zone will occur as rain or a mixture of rain and wet snow. Farther west, the cold air is likely to sweep in fast enough, before moisture departs, to allow perhaps up to a few inches of snow to fall from the mountains of eastern Tennessee and western North Carolina to south-central Pennsylvania.
